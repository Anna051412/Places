Chapter I

A Thousand
Years Ago

The king didn't want immortality. That's the lie history tells to make it sound noble.

What he really wanted was control over death itself. So he built the palace using stone dragged from the deepest parts of the ocean—stone that divers refused to touch because it felt warm.

Like it had a pulse.

Inside those walls, they didn't just perform rituals. They fed something. Prisoners. Travelers. Even their own people. One by one. Their screams didn't echo. The palace swallowed them.

No war. No poison. No visible cause.

Just a single night where every living person in the palace died at the exact same moment.

Their bodies weren't just frozen in fear. Their mouths were open. Wider than humanly possible. As if something had reached inside them and pulled something out.

The palace was not empty after that night.

It was just... waiting.

Chapter II

Ali Should Have
Drowned

Ali didn't escape the island the first time. That wasn't real. The island doesn't let go. It just lets you think you left.

So it can pull you back properly. Stronger. Deeper.

This time, when he stepped onto the island—he felt it immediately. Something was wrong with his body. His heartbeat wasn't steady. It was syncing.

With something under the ground.

Thump...
Thump...
Thump...

Not his heart. The island's.

The Palace Is Not Empty

The Doors

The doors were not open. They breathed. Slowly expanding... shrinking... like lungs.

Ali did not want to go inside. But his legs moved anyway. Every step felt like walking deeper into a throat.

Inside, the walls were wet. Not with water. Something thicker. Darker. It moved slightly when he looked at it too long.

They Are Not Dead

The bodies were still there. Exactly where they died. But now they blinked.

Slow. Dry. Painful blinks.

One of them turned its head. The sound of its neck moving was wrong. Like breaking wood soaked in mud.

It looked straight at Ali. And whispered:

You took too long.

What the King Summoned

The throne room was not a room anymore. It was deeper than it should be. Like the floor had no bottom. Like space itself had been stretched.

And in the center—that thing.

Not standing. Not floating. Just existing in a way that made his eyes hurt.

It did not have a shape. But every time he looked away and back it had changed. More limbs. Fewer limbs. Faces. No faces.

But always the eyes. Too many. Too close. Even when far away.

The Truth You Do Not Want

It did not kill them. It emptied them. Their bodies are just containers now.

And the palace? It is not a building. It is a mouth.

Ali tried to scream. Nothing came out. Because something was already inside him.

He felt it move slowly like something stretching after a long sleep.

A thought entered his mind. Not his own. Clear. Cold. Hungry.

You saw me once. That was enough.

The Worst Part

If you have read this far, you understand. The palace does not exist in space. It exists in the moment you become aware of it.

And awareness, once given, cannot be taken back.

The Throne Room

Ali stood in the throne room, and the thing before him was indescribable. Not standing. Not floating. Just existing in a way that made his eyes hurt.

It did not have a shape. But every time he looked away and back, it had changed. More limbs. Fewer limbs. Faces. No faces.

But always the eyes. Too many. Too close. Even when far away.

The creature leaned closer. Not with movement. With presence. The air itself bent toward him.

You have seen me. You are mine now. But you have a choice. You always do.

The Transcended

You open yourself completely. The boundaries between you and the creature dissolve like salt in water.

You are no longer Ali. You are not the creature either. You are something new. Something that exists in the spaces between thought and reality.

The palace is no longer a prison. It is home. You understand now what the king discovered a thousand years ago. This is not death. This is transformation.

And from this new existence, you see all the other versions of yourself, all the other Alis who made different choices. They are all here. They are all you.

You smile. Or perhaps the palace smiles through you. There is no difference anymore.

The palace remembers you. And you will remember everyone who comes after.

[ENDING: THE TRANSCENDED] You have achieved unity with the palace. You are no longer bound by the rules of singular existence.
Ending II of IV

The Freed

The palace screams. Reality tears. You are expelled like a foreign body rejected by an immune system.

You wake on a beach. Real sand. Real water. Real sky. The palace is gone. You are alone.

But something is wrong. The world feels flat now. Colors are muted. People seem like shadows. You can see through them to something beyond.

You are free. But freedom has a cost. You have seen beyond the veil, and you can never unsee it.

Sometimes, on quiet nights, you hear it calling. The palace. The creature. Yourself.

And you wonder if escape was really freedom, or just a different kind of prison.

[ENDING: THE FREED] You have escaped the palace, but the palace will never escape you. Freedom comes with the burden of knowledge.
Ending III of IV

The Consumed

You surrender. You let go. The creature flows into you, and you flow into it.

There is no pain. There is no fear. There is only the slow dissolution of self.

You become a container. Your body stands and walks, but it is not you anymore. It is the palace wearing your skin.

You are still aware, somewhere deep inside. You can feel yourself being stored, preserved, observed.

You are one of many. Thousands of versions of yourself, all contained within this single body, this single palace.

And when the next person arrives, you will welcome them. Because you are the palace now. And the palace is always hungry.

[ENDING: THE CONSUMED] You have become part of the palace's collection. Your consciousness persists, but your individuality is gone.
Ending IV of IV

The Merged

You fight. You resist. You run. And in doing so, you create a crack in the palace's hold on you.

But the creature does not let go. Instead, it clings to you as you flee. You are bound together now, two entities struggling for control of a single body.

You escape to the surface, but you are not alone. The creature is still inside you, still fighting for dominance.

You are neither fully human nor fully other. You are something in between. A hybrid. A paradox.

Some days you are in control. Some days it is. And on the worst days, you are both in control simultaneously, experiencing reality from two perspectives at once.

You are cursed and blessed. You are alone and never alone. You are the bridge between worlds.

[ENDING: THE MERGED] You have achieved a fragile balance between human and other. Neither fully free nor fully consumed.
